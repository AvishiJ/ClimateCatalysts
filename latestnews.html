Latest Updates

China continues to lead the way in renewable energy

China is exceeding its goal of 33% renewable electricity by 2025, contributing to almost 70% of global wind energy growth in 2021. Despite emitting a third of the world's greenhouse gases, China aims to reduce its carbon footprint, set to surpass its renewable targets by 50% by 2030. Only Gansu and Xinjiang lagged behind in meeting provincial renewable energy targets in 2021. Strategic efforts include large-scale desert renewable energy facilities and a substantial Taiwan Strait offshore wind farm capable of powering 13 million households.

Rising costs could hold back the future of renewable energy

In 2022, soaring material costs, including a 400% increase in PV-grade polysilicon, presented challenges for new renewable projects, driving up onshore wind facility construction costs by up to 25%. Despite geopolitical tensions impacting fossil fuel production costs, renewables maintained competitiveness. IEA data indicates a likely stabilization of renewable energy developments in 2023 amidst market instability.

Offshore wind energy is set to increase its share

In 2021, 93% of newly installed wind farm capacity was onshore, but there's a growing shift towards offshore wind energy. Countries like Canada, the US, India, China, and the UK are expanding offshore capabilities as technology advances. The Dogger Bank Wind Farm, underway in the UK, is set to surpass the Hornsea wind farm as the world's largest upon completion. Although offshore technology contributed 22% to total wind capacity growth in 2021, the rate is expected to plateau in 2023, with fewer projects coming online.

Solar energy will still be a dominating market

The solar PV capacity is poised for substantial growth, projected to surpass a terawatt of global solar power generation by 2023, constituting almost 60% of renewable growth. Despite rising costs, solar PV remains more cost-effective than other energy production methods. Leading nations in solar industry development include China, the US, Japan, Germany, and India, with various global projects underway. In 2022, renewables accounted for 83% of the world's power capacity, with solar energy leading at 22%. The UK, in particular, has experienced significant solar power growth, with an expected 4% compound annual growth rate, reaching 15,674 MW in 2023.

Biofuel demand will remain high

The conflict in Ukraine has significantly impacted biofuel costs due to higher feedstock prices and increased diesel costs. The IEA adjusted its biofuel demand growth forecast to 3% in 2023, reflecting ongoing instability from the conflict. Despite market disruptions, an OECD report predicts a 3% year-on-year increase in biofuel demand in 2023, mainly for transport. However, Biofuels International anticipates a 5% global biofuel demand rise, citing government policies in the US, Brazil, Europe, India, and Indonesia as key contributors, with renewable diesel and biodiesel expected to constitute two-thirds of this demand.

Energy Storage is a growing market

Energy security will be a key focus globally in 2023, linked to the continued growth of the electric vehicle (EV) market. It is estimated that there will be 836 million EVs in circulation, and the infrastructure and technology to power these vehicles will follow this upwards trend. Bloomberg has estimated that growth for domestic energy storage batteries to power EVs will rise to 155 GWh by 2030, growing 30% annually.

Renewable energy: predictions beyond are unreliable

The global energy market faces heightened volatility due to the global recession, energy crises, climate policy shifts, and conflicts, making predictions beyond 2023 challenging. Despite technological and policy advancements in renewables, rising costs and fluctuating demand present ongoing challenges. European nations aim to reduce dependence on imported energy, China works towards meeting climate goals, and the US pursues policies to decrease reliance on fossil fuels. Amid financial challenges, countries seek cost-effective renewable energy solutions to address climate change.
